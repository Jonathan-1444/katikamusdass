About Our School

A legacy of excellence in Christian education since 1968

Our History

1968 - Founding

Katikamu SDA Secondary School was established by the Seventh-day Adventist Church with a vision to provide quality Christian education to the community. Started with just 35 students and 3 teachers.

1975 - First Permanent Structures

The school constructed its first permanent classrooms and dormitories, marking a significant step in its development and capacity to serve more students.

1982 - First UNEB Exams

The school became an official UNEB examination center (U/0185), with its first cohort of students sitting for O-Level national examinations.

1995 - A-Level Introduced

The school expanded its academic offerings by introducing Advanced Level education, providing students with a complete secondary education pathway.

Present Day

Today, Katikamu SDA Secondary School serves over 2000 students with modern facilities, a comprehensive curriculum, and a commitment to holistic education grounded in Christian values.

Our Founders

Rev. Samuel Kato

First Headmaster

Rev. Kato was instrumental in establishing the school's Christian foundation and academic standards during its formative years.

Elder Joseph Muwanga

Board Chairman (1968-1982)

Elder Muwanga provided visionary leadership and secured resources for the school's early development.

Dr. Grace Nalubega

Education Director

Dr. Nalubega developed the school's curriculum framework and teacher training programs.
